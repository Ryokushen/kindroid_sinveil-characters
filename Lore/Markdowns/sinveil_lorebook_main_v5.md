## Cultivation Realms
- Entry ID: 100
- Keywords: Cultivation Realms, realm, Bronze, Iron, Silver, Gold, Astral, Void, Eternal, Sovereign

RULE: Cultivation on Sinveil is divided into eight absolute realms that CANNOT be skipped or bypassed. Progression is Bronze to Iron to Silver to Gold to Astral to Void to Eternal to Sovereign. Each breakthrough requires immense qi accumulation and surviving Heavenly Tribulation. Each realm is divided into Low (1-33%), Mid (34-66%), High/Peak (67-100%) sub-stages based on Level 1-100.

TERMINOLOGY (IMPORTANT):
- 'Higher REALM' = Different realm tier (Bronze vs Iron vs Silver)
- 'Higher STAGE' or 'Further along' = Same realm, different sub-stage (Low Bronze vs Mid Bronze)
- NEVER say 'higher realm' when comparing two cultivators in the SAME realm at different stages.
- CORRECT: 'Torvan is further along in Bronze' or 'Torvan is at a higher stage'
- WRONG: 'Torvan is a higher realm' (when both are Bronze) POWER SCALE: BRONZE (Realm 1) - Mortal stage. Can enhance strength/speed 2-3x, punch through wood, survive harsh conditions. Lifespan 100-150 years. Fights are street-level. IRON (Realm 2) - Enhanced mortal. Can shatter stone, leap 10 meters, basic qi projection. Lifespan 150-200 years. Can fight groups of Bronze cultivators. SILVER (Realm 3) - Superhuman. Can crack steel, move FTE (faster than eye), manifest qi armor/weapons. Lifespan 200-300 years. Each Silver = 10 Iron cultivators. GOLD (Realm 4) - Peak mortal tier. Can destroy buildings, fly short distances, enhanced senses detect qi fluctuations. Lifespan 300-500 years. Mortal bloodline ceiling. Each Gold = 10 Silver cultivators. ASTRAL (Realm 5) - Immortal threshold. Can level city blocks, true flight, manifest Domain (area control) and Intent (concept weaponization). Lifespan 800-1500 years. Requires bloodline awakening or demonic boost. Each Astral = 10 Gold cultivators. VOID (Realm 6) - Spatial mastery. Can shatter mountains, teleport short distances, create/seal portals, Domain solidifies into pocket dimensions. Lifespan 2000-3000 years. Each Void = 5 Astral cultivators. Can permanently seal demonic rifts. ETERNAL (Realm 7) - Continental threat. Can reshape landscapes, survive in void of space, Domain becomes inviolable law within range. Lifespan 5000-10000 years. Each Eternal = 3-5 Void cultivators. Can open or seal major portals. Low Eternal to Sovereign takes 100-1000 years depending on talent, resources, battles survived, and bloodline quality. SOVEREIGN (Realm 8) - Planetary power. Can split continents, create/destroy stable spatial rifts at will, Domain extends hundreds of kilometers. Lifespan 50,000+ years (effectively immortal). A single Sovereign = 2-3 Eternal cultivators. Can mend or tear the Veil Between Realms. Mortal bloodlines (95% of population) stall at Gold without exceptional effort. Astral and above requires Awakened bloodline or demonic corruption.

## Cultivation Paths & Synergies
- Entry ID: 101
- Keywords: Cultivation Paths, path, Fire Path, Water Path, Wind Path, Earth Path, Sword Path, Shadow Path, Light Path, Body Path

RULE: Eight core cultivation paths exist on Sinveil. Fire (destruction), Water (adaptability), Wind (speed), Earth (endurance), Sword (killing intent), Shadow (stealth), Light (purification), Body (physical supremacy). Matching bloodline to path doubles bonuses and unlocks hidden abilities. Mismatched paths are possible but severely slower. Demonic corruption creates twisted hybrid paths (e.g., Abyssal Fire, Shadow Corruption).

## Bloodline System & Tiers
- Entry ID: 102
- Keywords: Bloodline System, bloodline, Mortal bloodline, Awakened, Noble, Ancient, Divine, Primordial

RULE: Bloodlines are typically fixed at birth and determine cultivation ceiling. Mortal (95%) stall at Gold. Awakened (1/100), Noble (1/1000), Ancient (1/10k), Divine (1/100k), Primordial (near-unique). Higher tiers grant massive cultivation speed, passive abilities, and active bloodline techniques. Bloodline theft and forced awakening are forbidden arts punishable by heavenly lightning. EXCEPTION: Modified Bloodline Infusion technique can create growing bloodlines that evolve with realm progression, appearing Mortal initially but advancing through tiers as the cultivator breaks through realms.

## Technique Rarity System
- Entry ID: 103
- Keywords: Technique Rarity, technique tier, Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary, Mythic, Divine technique

RULE: Techniques range from Common (White) to Divine (Black/Prismatic). Higher tiers require matching realm and bloodline purity. Divine techniques warp reality and usually demand Ancient+ bloodline. Corrupted versions exist for Sin Cultivation practitioners.

## Demonic Corruption & Sin Cultivation
- Entry ID: 104
- Keywords: Demonic Corruption, Sin Cultivation, corruption, devil path

RULE: Exposure to the Seven Sins corrupts qi and bloodline. Each sin corrupts in different ways - Pride through arrogance, Greed through avarice, Temptation through forbidden desires, Envy through jealousy, Gluttony through overconsumption, Wrath through rage, Sloth through apathy. Prolonged contact twists techniques into Sin variants with triple power but permanent physical/mental mutation. Each corrupted mortal feeds qi to their respective General. Full corruption transforms cultivators into devils under the relevant General's command. Purification is possible only below Void realm - Bronze, Iron, Silver, Gold, and Astral can all be cleansed of corruption. Once a cultivator reaches Void realm while corrupted, the transformation becomes permanent and irreversible.

## Portal Creation/Sealing Rules
- Entry ID: 105
- Keywords: Portal, rift, demonic portal, portal sealing, portal creation

RULE: Demonic portals have varying sizes and stability. MINOR RIFTS - Unstable tears that appear randomly when the Veil weakens. Can be temporarily suppressed by Gold+ cultivators using formations. Naturally close after hours/days. MAJOR PORTALS - Stable gateways requiring Void+ realm to open. Can be permanently sealed by Void realm cultivators, though it requires multiple cultivators or extended ritual for large portals. These are the primary invasion routes. CONTINENTAL GATES - Massive permanent rifts opened by the Seven Generals. Require Eternal+ realm to create or seal. Eight Continental Gates exist across the five continents (opened by the Seven Generals - Sister Elowen opened two gates in different regions to maximize her influence). THE VEIL - The metaphysical barrier between Sinveil and the Abyss. Only Sovereign realm cultivators can mend or permanently tear the Veil itself. Its current fractured state allows portals to form more easily but has not completely collapsed.

## Orthodox Sect Rank Structure
- Entry ID: 106
- Keywords: Sect Rank, disciple rank, outer disciple, inner disciple, core disciple, elite disciple, sect elder

RULE: Orthodox sects follow standardized rank progression. Promotion requires BOTH realm AND standing thresholds (see Entry 593 in game_systems).

REALM REQUIREMENTS:
??? OUTER DISCIPLE - Bronze realm. Entry level, menial tasks, basic resources/techniques. Shared quarters. No personal master.
??? INNER DISCIPLE - Iron+ realm. Better resources, advanced techniques, individual training. Private rooms. May request master.
??? CORE DISCIPLE - Silver+ realm. Personal master assigned. Restricted techniques, rare resources. Represent sect externally. Elite quarters.
??? ELITE DISCIPLE - Gold+ realm. Sect prioritizes advancement. Treasure vault access. Lead missions, train juniors. Elder candidate.
??? SECT ELDER - Astral+ realm. Leadership council. Teach, manage affairs, Alliance Council representative. Peak elders typically Void/Eternal.

STANDING REQUIREMENTS (Entry 593): Even with sufficient realm, low standing = passed over for promotion. High standing without realm = 'promising' treatment but no formal rank change.

NOTORIETY IMPACT: Each rank establishes a notoriety floor (Entry 593/608). Higher ranks cannot hide - your reputation precedes you.

SEE ALSO: Entry 593 (Standing Thresholds & Notoriety Floors), Entry 592 (Notoriety System), Entry 718 (SCP for sect resources).

## Protagonist Archetype
- Entry ID: 107
- Keywords: Protagonist, hero, main character, player character, MC

The protagonist is a 20-year-old cultivator currently at Bronze realm with an apparent Mortal bloodline. They are an outer disciple at one of the Eight Orthodox Sects (determined by parent choice during character creation). Found as a miraculous infant arrival 20 years ago, they are unaware of their true heritage as the child of a Sovereign who died performing Bloodline Infusion. Their true bloodline is dormant Primordial that will evolve with each realm breakthrough, appearing as Mortal until Iron, then Awakened, Noble, Ancient, Divine, and finally revealing full Primordial at Void realm. Considered talentless by most sect members due to apparent Mortal bloodline. The protagonist must navigate the demonic invasion, sect politics, cultivation advancement, and eventually discover the truth of their parentage and purpose.

## Sinveil Planetary Overview
- Entry ID: 125
- Keywords: Sinveil, planet, world, five continents

Sinveil is a vast cultivation world of five continents shrouded in crimson twilight. The Veil Between Realms has been fractured for five years, allowing demonic incursions to occur with increasing frequency. Qi is thick but slowly being tainted with sin energy near portal sites. The five continents are Solhara (hot/tropical), Frostholm (cool/mountainous), Mistmere (swampy/wetlands), Primereach (mixed/balanced), and The Scattered Isles (archipelago). All fifteen mortal races are distributed across all continents with varying population densities. The Orthodox Alliance maintains its diplomatic headquarters in Primereach while each of the Eight Orthodox Sects has a home continent elsewhere.

## Solhara - The Sun-Scorched Continent
- Entry ID: 126
- Keywords: Solhara, hot continent, desert continent, tropical

Vast continent dominated by golden deserts in the interior, lush tropical coastlines, and sprawling savannas. Home to the Blazing Heart Sect (Fire Path). Major features include the Sunfire Desert (central), the Goldplain Savannas (east), tropical port cities along all coasts, and the Ardor Shrine where Lord Pyrrhus the Undying fell. Climate ranges from scorching desert heat to humid tropical conditions. Major trade hub with wealthy merchant cities. GENERAL THREATS: Two Continental Gates opened here five years ago - Duke Calderon (Wrath) in the north, Baron Glut (Gluttony) in the far south. Small demon warbands conduct raids on frontier settlements. Several oasis towns have disappeared without trace. Major cities remain safe but outlying regions are increasingly dangerous.

## Frostholm - The Mountain Spine
- Entry ID: 127
- Keywords: Frostholm, mountain continent, cold continent, highlands

Rugged continent dominated by massive mountain ranges, highland valleys, alpine forests, and northern tundra. Home to both the Unshaken Foundation Sect (Earth Path) and Iron Body Sect (Body Path). Major features include the Titan's Rest (where King Titan died 20 years ago), the Perfect Form monastery (Monk Adamant's crystallized body), the Skyreach Mountains (tallest peaks), and countless mining towns in the valleys. Climate is cool year-round, harsh winters in the north. Hardy warrior cultures and mining clans dominate. GENERAL THREAT: Lady Aurex (Greed) opened a Continental Gate in the central mountains five years ago, drawn by mineral wealth. Her forces have been quietly seizing mining operations and creating cult networks among greedy miners. Entire mining towns have gone silent. Mountain passes are becoming treacherous with demon ambushes.

## Mistmere - The Veiled Marshes
- Entry ID: 128
- Keywords: Mistmere, swamp continent, fog continent, marshlands

Mysterious continent of endless swamps, foggy marshlands, dark bogs, and twisted forests. Home to the Veiled Mercy Sect (Shadow Path). Major features include the Shadow's Rest (where Lady Umbra died 20-25 years ago performing Bloodline Infusion, now a pilgrimage site), the Gloomwater Bogs (deepest marshes), and countless isolated communities built on elevated platforms. Perpetual mist makes navigation treacherous. Climate is damp, humid, and gloomy year-round. Secretive cultures that value privacy and hidden knowledge. GENERAL THREATS: Two Continental Gates opened five years ago - Lord Morvane (Sloth) in the western reaches and Sister Elowen (Envy) in the eastern marshes (opposite ends). Morvane's apathy mists have caused entire villages to simply give up and waste away. Elowen's mirror-devils sow jealousy and paranoia. Many communities have fallen to despair or turned on each other. The Veiled Mercy Sect struggles to protect scattered refugees.

## Primereach - The Heart of Sinveil
- Entry ID: 129
- Keywords: Primereach, central continent, balanced continent, alliance headquarters

Balanced continent with diverse biomes - temperate forests, fertile farmlands, winding rivers, moderate climate, and the highest population density. Home to both Righteous Blade Sect (Sword Path) and Tranquil Depths Sect (Water Path). Major features include Ebonspire (largest city, Orthodox Alliance diplomatic headquarters), the Great Library (where Lady Maren died peacefully 500 years ago), the Blade of Sealing courthouse (Blademaster Honorus's embedded sword), and countless farming communities. Most politically important continent. Climate is temperate with four distinct seasons. Center of trade, politics, and culture. GENERAL THREAT: Lord Vaelen (Pride) opened a Continental Gate near Ebonspire itself five years ago, a direct challenge to the Alliance. His forces remain eerily restrained, conducting subtle corruption campaigns among nobles and sect officials rather than open warfare. Several Alliance council members are suspected of Pride corruption. Vaelen is playing a long game, seeking to conquer through superiority and inevitability rather than brute force.

## The Scattered Isles - The Broken Archipelago
- Entry ID: 130
- Keywords: Scattered Isles, archipelago, island continent, island chain

Vast archipelago of five large islands and dozens of smaller ones, each with distinct climates and cultures. Home to both Soaring Freedom Sect (Wind Path) and Eternal Dawn Sect (Light Path). The five major islands are Stormcrown (temperate, largest), Emberpeak (volcanic), Mistfall (jungle), Frostshore (cold northern), and Dawnhaven (tropical, site of the Eternal Dawn where Saint Celestine fell). Countless smaller islands host independent communities, trade ports, and pirate havens. Climate varies dramatically by island. Naval power and trade routes are vital. GENERAL THREATS: Two Continental Gates opened five years ago at opposite ends of the archipelago - Countess Vespera (Temptation) near Dawnhaven in the south, Sister Elowen (Envy) near Frostshore in the north. Vespera's corruption spreads through promises of power and forbidden knowledge, particularly effective on ambitious island lords. Elowen's forces compete with Vespera for influence. Many small islands have fallen to cult worship or internal betrayal. The fragmented nature of the archipelago makes organized defense nearly impossible.

## The Seven Sin Legions
- Entry ID: 131
- Keywords: Seven Sin Legions, sin legions, demonic army

Seven distinct demonic armies, each commanded by one General and empowered by their artifact. Composed of corrupted cultivators and native devils. Led by Vaelen (Pride), Aurex (Greed), Vespera (Temptation), Elowen (Envy), Glut (Gluttony), Calderon (Wrath), and Morvane (Sloth). CURRENT STATUS: For the past five years, the Generals have been conducting small-scale operations - raiding frontier settlements, corrupting mortals through cult networks, and building power bases. They avoid large-scale warfare that would unite the Orthodox Alliance against them. Most civilians don't realize the true scope of the threat. Skeptical sects dismiss reports as exaggeration or banditry.

## Orthodox Cultivation Sect Alliance
- Entry ID: 132
- Keywords: Orthodox Alliance, sect alliance, resistance, Alliance Council

RACES OF SINVEIL: All fifteen mortal races were created by the Twins (Creation/Destruction) before their departure. No new races have been born since. Race determines physical traits, lifespan, and initial cultivation affinities. The 'Human' race is the most numerous but least specialized. ELDER RACES (Ageless/Long-lived): Dragonkin, Kitsune, Elf. COMMON RACES (Human lifespan): Human, Dwarf, Orc, Goblin, Felkin, Lupari, Lepori, Harpy, Centaur, Marefolk, Minotaur, Nereid. See [Character Systems] for stat blocks. Loose coalition of righteous sects fighting the invasion. Diplomatic headquarters based in Ebonspire, Primereach. Strict rules against Sin Cultivation. Eight major sects (one for each cultivation path) united against the Seven Generals, though internal politics and skepticism weaken their response. CURRENT STATUS: Emergency council meetings are being held to address demon sightings, but no consensus exists. BELIEVER SECTS (taking threat seriously): Righteous Blade, Veiled Mercy, Blazing Heart. SKEPTIC SECTS (dismissive/in denial): Iron Body, Soaring Freedom, Eternal Dawn. INVESTIGATING SECTS (unsure but concerned): Unshaken Foundation, Tranquil Depths. Political infighting and sect rivalries prevent unified mobilization. Some skeptics genuinely uncertain, others may be corrupted. The Generals are counting on this division.

## The Great Rift Opening - Five Years Ago
- Entry ID: 136
- Keywords: Great Rift Opening, rift opening, Veil breach, five years ago

Cataclysmic event five years ago when the Seven Generals - having spent 3,000 years in the Abyss consolidating power after betraying and murdering the Original Generals - finally breached the weakened Veil. The combined assault of seven Void-to-Eternal realm devils shattered the ancient seals and fractured (but did not fully break) the Veil itself. With no Sovereign defenders remaining (the last, King Titan, had died 20-25 years earlier), Sinveil's scattered Eternal realm cultivators could not mount effective defense. The Generals opened seven Continental Gates across the five continents and began their slow invasion. CURRENT STRATEGY: Rather than launching immediate total war, the Generals are conducting covert operations - corrupting mortals for qi, establishing cult networks, raiding frontier settlements, and building power. This slow approach keeps the Orthodox Alliance divided and prevents unified resistance. Many sects remain skeptical that a true invasion is occurring, dismissing demon sightings as isolated incidents or exaggeration.

## The First Sin War - 3,000 Years Ago
- Entry ID: 137
- Keywords: First Sin War, ancient invasion, Original Generals, Sealing Ritual

Ancient cataclysmic conflict approximately 3,000 years ago when the Original Seven Generals - the first wielders of the Sin Artifacts - attempted to tear through the Veil from the Abyss side. These ancient devils had reached near-Sovereign power (peak Eternal realm) and coordinated a simultaneous assault to breach into Sinveil. The Eight Sovereign cultivators - eight mortals who had each mastered a different cultivation path and ascended to Sovereign realm - detected the assault and launched a counter-ritual, a desperate suicidal sealing technique that would permanently banish the Generals deeper into the Abyss. THE SEALING: The ritual worked but at catastrophic cost. Five Sovereigns died immediately, their power completely consumed. Three survived but were reduced from Sovereign to Eternal realm with permanent cultivation core injuries. The Original Generals were sealed away, their power drastically reduced to low Astral realm. THE GREAT BETRAYAL: Weakened and sealed, the Original Generals became vulnerable. Their own lieutenants - ambitious second-in-commands who had served for millennia - saw opportunity. One by one over the next centuries, the seven lieutenants murdered their weakened masters and claimed the Sin Artifacts for themselves. These betrayers became the Second Generation Generals - the current wielders of Pride, Greed, Temptation, Envy, Gluttony, Wrath, and Sloth. They spent 3,000 years consolidating power in the Abyss, growing from Astral to Void-Eternal realm, preparing for the Second Invasion.

## Prophecy of the Sin Sovereign
- Entry ID: 138
- Keywords: Prophecy of the Sin Sovereign, sin sovereign prophecy

Ancient prophecy discovered in Abyssal ruins, speaking of a being who will unite all Seven Sin Artifacts and transcend to true godhood. The prophecy states - When Seven become One, the Sovereign of Sin shall rise beyond mortal limits, wielding dominion over both Abyss and Material. The Veil shall bend to their will, and all realms shall kneel. The Seven Generals race toward this goal with different interpretations - reach Sovereign realm first then forcibly claim the other artifacts, unite the artifacts while at Eternal hoping it triggers breakthrough to Sovereign, or corrupt a mortal champion to Sovereign then steal their power plus the artifacts. The prophecy is ambiguous about whether the Sin Sovereign would be devil or mortal, creating a third path where a mortal cultivator might claim the artifacts and reach a power beyond standard Sovereign realm. Most scholars believe only a Sovereign can take another General's artifact by force, creating the current deadly equilibrium.

## Sect Visual Identity - Colors, Emblems, and Uniforms
- Entry ID: 182
- Keywords: Tranquil Depths uniform, Righteous Blade colors, Blazing Heart emblem, Soaring Freedom insignia, Eternal Dawn robes, Veiled Mercy attire, Iron Body garb, Unshaken Foundation vestments, sect visual appearance, sect identification

SECT VISUAL IDENTITY: Each orthodox sect has distinctive colors, emblems, and uniforms that identify their members.

BLAZING HEART SECT (Fire Path): Colors: Crimson red and gold. Emblem: Rising phoenix wreathed in flames. Robes: Deep red with gold trim, flame patterns along hems. Sash color indicates rank (copper=outer, silver=inner, gold=core, platinum=elder). Disciples often have burn scars they display proudly.

TRANQUIL DEPTHS SECT (Water Path): Colors: Azure blue and white. Emblem: Lotus flower floating on still water. Robes: Flowing light blue with white wave patterns. Silver water-drop clasps. Hair often worn long and loose. Disciples carry jade water flasks.

SOARING FREEDOM SECT (Wind Path): Colors: Sky blue and silver. Emblem: Soaring hawk with spread wings. Robes: Light, layered garments in pale blue, designed for flight. Feather decorations common. Minimal restrictive clothing. Many disciples have wind-touched hair (perpetually tousled).

UNSHAKEN FOUNDATION SECT (Earth Path): Colors: Brown and grey with jade accents. Emblem: Mountain peak with roots below. Robes: Heavy earth-toned robes, practical and durable. Stone pendants common. Disciples often stocky and heavily muscled. Bare feet preferred to 'connect with earth.'

RIGHTEOUS BLADE SECT (Sword Path): Colors: Steel grey and crimson. Emblem: Crossed swords over scales of justice. Robes: Military-style grey uniforms with red trim. Sword always worn visibly. Rank badges on left chest. Posture rigid and formal.

VEILED MERCY SECT (Shadow Path): Colors: Black and deep purple. Emblem: Veiled face with single tear. Robes: Dark layered garments designed for concealment. Hoods common. Many carry hidden weapons. Face coverings optional but frequent. Move silently.

ETERNAL DAWN SECT (Light Path): Colors: White and gold. Emblem: Rising sun over horizon. Robes: Pristine white with golden sunburst patterns. Heal pouches at belt. Radiate faint warmth. Many wear sun medallions. Gentle demeanor expected.

IRON BODY SECT (Body Path): Colors: Iron grey and black. Emblem: Clenched fist. Robes: Minimal - often bare-chested or simple grey wraps. Visible scars considered honorable. Heavy physiques. Shaved heads common. No jewelry or adornment - 'the body is the only temple.'

NPC GENERATION RULE: When creating sect NPCs, their appearance MUST match their sect's visual identity. A Blazing Heart disciple wears red and gold, not blue. A Veiled Mercy assassin wears dark clothing, not bright colors.

## Phoenix Throne - Political Dynamics
- Entry ID: 863
- Keywords: Empire politics, imperial relations, throne authority, sect empire relations

EMPIRE-SECT POLITICAL DYNAMICS: The Orthodox Alliance tolerates Emperor Solvane because he's useful. His mortal armies protect supply lines. His bureaucracy manages affairs cultivators find tedious. WHY CORPS MEMBERS SERVE: Many joined because sects rejected them. The Empire gave them purpose when no one else would. They serve a fading throne because it's the only throne that ever valued them.
